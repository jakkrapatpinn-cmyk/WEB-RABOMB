ORIGIN FILE

The Big W. Story

Every great collection needs a villain origin. Here's ours — written by the three people who run this warehouse and refuse to apologize for it.

HOW IT STARTED

From Garage Sale to Garage Co.

2019

A motorcycle, a storage unit, and bad intentions

Big W. Garage started as three friends flipping bootleg pins out of a storage unit. The pins sold out in a weekend. The lesson stuck: scarcity sells, especially to people who want to take what isn't theirs.

2021

First capsule, first sellout, first apology email (unsent)

The Garlic Engine capsule moved 200 units in four minutes. We never sent the "we're sorry it sold out" email, because we weren't sorry. That became the brand voice.

2023

The warehouse upgrade

Outgrew the storage unit. Moved into an actual garage with actual gold-plated hardware suppliers. Started numbering every piece, because greed deserves receipts.

2026

Four worlds, one philosophy

Apparel, headwear, accessories, collectibles — four product worlds, one rule: if it doesn't feel worth hoarding, it doesn't ship.

WHAT WE WON'T COMPROMISE ON

House Rules

🪙

Scarcity, Real Not Fake

Every "limited run" number is true. When a run says 200, exactly 200 exist — we shred the molds after.

🧵

Heavy Or Nothing

If a fabric feels like a festival freebie, it doesn't make the line. Everything is built to outlast the hype cycle.

🛠️

No Filler Drops

We'd rather ship four pieces a season we're proud of than twelve we're not. Quantity is not the flex.

🤝

Parody, Not Theft

We're a fan-run tribute label. No official branding, no pretending to be something we're not — just love for a great in-game rival.

👑
FOUNDER / HEAD OF GARAGE

"We don't make merch. We make loot."

Started this label because I wanted a jacket heavy enough to feel like it was stolen from somewhere important. Three drops later, people keep asking where the "somewhere important" is. There isn't one. That's the bit.
